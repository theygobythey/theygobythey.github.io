Jaq Nguyen Victor Jaq is the whimsy-drenched child of a love-warrior mother to whom they credit all of their magic. This queer, genderqueer, Vietnamese unicorn is finishing up their graduate studies in Drama Therapy at the California Institute of Integral Studies (CIIS). They are also a massage therapist. Jaq adores galloping around the Bay area doing things like: social justice stand up comedy with Peacock Rebellion, teaching art with the Diasporic Vietnamese Artists Network, youth programming with Blackgirl Dangerous, em-ceeing with the Asian Pacific Islander Queer Women & Trans Community, mentoring at the Gender Spectrum Conference, facilitation at the Queer Asian Conference, eco-justice healing with the Work that Reconnects, non-violent direct action with the Buddhist Peace Fellowship, and more. This year, Jaq will premiere their first short film, Swanicorn: A Genderqueer Fairytale, at the Queer Women of Color Media Arts Project Film Festival and a short performance piece called Decolonization is Like Hard at Stanfordâ€™s Soul Wound Conference. Last year, they debuted their first full-length show, Gender Assimilation: A Rebuttal, at the United States of Asian America Festival. Currently, Jaq is working on two plays, Princess Genderqueer & the Racist Pea and The Unicorn Genome Project. Soon, they will be directing a troupe called QD Play: Queering & Decolonizing Playback and a training called Dig & Demand: Diasporic Vietnamese Artists for Justice.

Gender Assimilation: A Rebuttal What is it to be genderqueer? In an intimate portrayal, Jaq Victor gives themself over to theatrical investigation in a debut solo show. Their butt serves as staging ground upon which to expose and push back against social narratives around racism, beauty privilege, transphobia, horizontal oppression, and so much more.
